VeritablAI

Misinformation is abundant on the Internet, and many political campaigns utilize it for their benefit, which is damaging to people's lives and interpersonal relationships. This is where our product, VeritablAI, comes in. It analyzes speech from a person and checks it against a Wikipedia database to determine if their talking points are scientifically accurate. Then, in real time, a banner for how truthful the assertion is will constantly update, depending on its accuracy. That way, for instance, viewers at home can determine if a politician is distorting facts if this were to be deployed on a TV show.

Most of our code was done in Python. We used FastAPI for our web server, Vultr to store our data and run some of our code constantly, Snowflake API's LLM capabilities to parse recorded audio, and Google Gemini to compare the audio sample with cached Wikipedia pages for accuracy.
